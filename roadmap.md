# Interpretation and claims

## Supported claim

A sparse, cost-aware tenor allocator can improve the risk-adjusted profile of macro-anchored 10Y duration timing.

## Not claimed

This repository does not claim pure relative-value alpha. The main strategies retain meaningful exposure to duration-risk premia and should be interpreted as allocation engines, not market-neutral curve RV trades.

## Why the result matters

The result supports a broader portfolio-construction principle: sparse/greedy allocation can be portable outside the original strategic-allocation setting. Here it works in a rates-native universe where assets are curve tenors rather than asset classes.

## Correct label

Use:

> macro-anchored sparse curve allocation

Do not use:

> pure yield-curve relative value
